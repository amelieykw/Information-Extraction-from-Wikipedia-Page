<entity name="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Olivier_Grouillard">Olivier</entity> <entity name="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Olivier_Grouillard">Grouillard</entity> (born 2 September 1958 in Fenouillet) is a racing driver from France. He started racing go-karts from the age of fourteen competing in events such as the Volant Elf. He progressed to Formula Renault winning the title before <entity name="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Olivier_Grouillard">Grouillard</entity> competed in F3000 from 1985 to 1988 taking two wins. He also participated in the Birmingham Superprix but did not start the race.

In 1989, <entity name="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Olivier_Grouillard">Grouillard</entity> joined the Ligier Formula One team before joining the Osella team for 1990 and 1991 when the team was renamed Fondmetal. His last season came in 1992, when <entity name="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Olivier_Grouillard">Grouillard</entity> drove for Tyrrell. He left the sport following the year without receiving offers for a drive in 1993. After leaving Formula One, <entity name="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Olivier_Grouillard">Grouillard</entity> raced in the CART PPG World Series, competing in the Indianapolis 500 for which he did not qualify but showed consistency. <entity name="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Olivier_Grouillard">Grouillard</entity> became well known for driving in Sports Cars between 1994 and 2001 having success. During his time in Sports Cars, he raced in the BPR Global GT Series, the FIA GT Championship, the Daytona 24 Hours, the Le Mans 24 Hours and the American Le Mans Series. He retired from motorsport at the end of 2001.

Career
Pre-Formula One

<entity name="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Olivier_Grouillard">Grouillard</entity> started racing karts from the age of 14 and competed in the Volant Elf in 1981 and progressed to Formula Renault in 1982 and took the title. In 1983, <entity name="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Olivier_Grouillard">Grouillard</entity> went into French F3 and drove for the ORECA team racing a Martini Mk39 Alfa Romeo to finish 4th scoring 33 points. Driving a Martini Mk42 brought him the title and scored 108 points. For 1985, <entity name="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Olivier_Grouillard">Grouillard</entity> competed in the inaugural season of F3000 spending the year mostly confined to qualifying in the midfield but delivered good races along with reliability. In 1986, he left ORECA to join Formula Team Ltd driving a Lola T86/50 Cosworth. At Mugello, <entity name="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Olivier_Grouillard">Grouillard</entity> finished 4th despite taking an early lead. At the Osterrichring he took 6th and overall scored 4 points despite limited participation as the team was short on funding. <entity name="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Olivier_Grouillard">Grouillard</entity> returned to ORECA for 1987 in a March 87B Cosworth. His performances dropped despite having impressive qualifying record throughout the season. For 1988, <entity name="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Olivier_Grouillard">Grouillard</entity> moved to the GBDA Motorsport driving a Lola T88/50 Cosworth. He once again showed potential during qualifying and was regularly scoring points along with wins.[1] <entity name="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Olivier_Grouillard">Grouillard</entity> was slightly injured in a crash during the 1988 Birmingham Superprix sustaining bruised ankles although observers initially thought that <entity name="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Olivier_Grouillard">Grouillard</entity> had broken both his legs. <entity name="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Olivier_Grouillard">Grouillard</entity> was able to start the race but had to run to the pit lane for the spare car after his car suffered a failure with the engine. <entity name="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Olivier_Grouillard">Grouillard</entity> however, did not start the race.[2]

<entity name="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Olivier_Grouillard">Olivier</entity> also raced a series of works and semi-works BMW M3's for BMW in the 1987 World Touring Car Championship and the 1988 European Touring Car Championships

1990

During the race, <entity name="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Olivier_Grouillard">Grouillard</entity> was hit by Riccardo Patrese's Williams at the second corner and was eventually retired after a second collision with the Swiss driver Gregor Foitek. At Adleaide, <entity name="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Olivier_Grouillard">Grouillard</entity> finished 13th and held up Nigel Mansell which led to the British driver shaking his fist at the Frenchman in front of the on-board camera.[3]
1991

<entity name="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Olivier_Grouillard">Grouillard</entity> remained with Osella which had been brought out and renamed by Fondmetal. 1992

<entity name="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Olivier_Grouillard">Grouillard</entity> was taken on by Ken Tyrrell to drive the Ilmor powered 020B chassis and was partnered by Andrea de Cesaris. <entity name="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Olivier_Grouillard">Grouillard</entity> was learning from de Cesaris' experience and often qualified in the midfield. His car had the share of mechanical problems but managed to finish in San Marino, Canada, France and Britain but a collision with a wall in Spain along with a collision Karl Wendlinger's March in Hungary, a spin on the opening lap at Spa, a collision with Pierluigi Martini's Dallara in Adelaide had brought himself unrepentant about his manners whilst racing. <entity name="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Olivier_Grouillard">Grouillard</entity> eventually was not wanted by other teams and left Formula One at the end of the season.[3] <entity name="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Olivier_Grouillard">Grouillard</entity> and other lead French drivers lobbied for leading Motor Sport events such as the French motorcycle Grand Prix to be saved with the newly enforced anti-tobacco laws in France which was brought in by Claude Evin.[4]
CART

<entity name="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Olivier_Grouillard">Grouillard</entity> arranged to drive with the Indy Regency Team in a Lola T92/00 for 1993 in the CART PPG World Series following Nigel Mansell in the series. His first race was the Indianapolis 500, where he did not qualify. After the 500, <entity name="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Olivier_Grouillard">Grouillard</entity> showed consistency and had a reliable car finishing in eight out of 11 events with only one mechanical retirement at Michigan and a collision with Arie Luyendyk at Vancouver. Overall, <entity name="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Olivier_Grouillard">Grouillard</entity> finished 28th overall taking four points.[5]
Sports Car racing
1994-2001

Unable to make a success in CART, he returned to Europe in 1994 and raced sports cars in the BPR Global GT series. He had previous experience in 1990 where he and co-drivers Martin Donnelly and Kenny Acheson qualified 5th at Le Mans but gearbox problems prevented the trio from starting. He came 16th in a Venturi 400 Trophy with Herve Pulain coming in 4th in GT5 class before driving five races in an Agusta Racing Team Venturi 600LM with Christophe Bouchut taking 2nd at Montlehry and 5th at Vallelunga and also raced at Le Mans in a Jacadi Racing Venturi 600Lm with Michel Fert√© and Michel Neugarten with engine problems ending their race.[5]

The pair were joined by Jean-Denis Deletraz at Le Mans, where they finished 5th. <entity name="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Olivier_Grouillard">Grouillard</entity> departed for Mach One Racing joining Andy Wallace winning all three rounds the new pairing entered. <entity name="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Olivier_Grouillard">Grouillard</entity> scored 91 points and finished 14th overall.[5] In the first qualifying race at Nogaro, <entity name="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Olivier_Grouillard">Grouillard</entity>'s car suffered a faulty shock absorber.[6]

He remained with Mach One, and Harrods came on board as sponsor. Retirements plagued the team for the first four rounds were remedied by victory at Silverstone and a 6th at Le Mans where they were joined by Derek Bell. Throughout the season, he also competed in other events such as the Daytona 24 Hours with Derek Hill and Gildo Pastor Pallanca with a transmission problem forcing the trio out.[5]

<entity name="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Olivier_Grouillard">Grouillard</entity> entered the FIA GT Championship in 1997 in a Dave Price Racing Panoz GTP Ford, teamed up with David Brabham and Perry McCarthy and also competed in Japanese GT finishing 2nd at Suzuka in a Toyota Supra with Masami Kageyama.
